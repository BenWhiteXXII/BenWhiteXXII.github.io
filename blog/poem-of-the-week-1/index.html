Poem of the Week - Ah Vastness of Pines by Pablo Neruda

2024-02-26

This is a new series of posts I’m going to try to stick to, if only to let me rediscover and rethink some of my favourite poetry. Another bonus is that it’ll keep this blog updated and alive.

Ah Vastness of Pines

Ah vastness of pines, murmur of waves breaking,

slow play of lights, solitary bell,

twilight falling in your eyes, toy doll,

earth-shell, in whom the earth sings!

In you the rivers sing and my soul flees in them

as you desire, and you send it where you will.

Aim my road on your bow of hope

and in a frenzy I will flee my flock of arrows.

On all sides I see your waist of fog,

and your silence hunts down my afflicted hours;

my kisses anchor, and my moist desire nests

in your arms of transparent stone.

Ah your mysterious voice that love tolls and darkens

in the resonant and dying evening!

Thus in the deep hours I have seen, over the fields,

the ears of wheat tolling in the mouth of the wind.

This poem is one of my favourite’s of Neruda and in my opinion makes for a perfect opening to this series. While I wasn’t attracted to all of his poems in the collection Twenty love poems and a song of despair I did love his descriptions of nature and the unique imagery they create, especially in this poem which he uses to create an isolating and almost apocolyptic atmosphere.
